About Covid-19

Coronavirus disease (COVID-19) is an infectious disease caused by a newly discovered coronavirus.
Most people infected with the COVID-19 virus will experience mild to moderate respiratory illness
Older people, and those with underlying medical problems like cardiovascular disease,
diabetes, chronic respiratory disease, and cancer are more likely to develop serious illness.

Stay Safe

Prevent and slow down transmission by staying well informed about the COVID-19 virus, the disease it causes and how it spreads.

Protect yourself and others from infection by washing your hands or using an alcohol based rub frequently and not touching your face.

Stay at least 2 metres (3 steps) away from anyone you do not live with or anyone not in your support bubble

Wear a mask that covers your nose and mouth to help protect yourself and others.

Symptoms

High Temperature

When you feel your chest and/or back and it feels hot to touch. Above 38 degrees

New Cough

A new, continuous cough: If you find yourself coughing for more than 1 hour, or if you have 3+ coughing episodes within 24 hours.
If you usually have a cough, it will be worse than usual.

Loss of taste

A loss or change to your sense of taste or smell: This either means that you cannot smell or taste anything, or things smell or taste different to normal

Breathlesness

Shortness of breath or difficulty in breathing

Vaccine

Getting vaccinated is one of many steps you can take to protect yourself and those around you from COVID-19.
The vaccine is given in 2 doses, roughly 3 -12 weeks apart. 2 doses is required for maximum protection against old and new variants.
It's important to get vaccinated once you have been offered a vaccine.

Eligibility

Anyone 30 and over and those in vunerable groups are entitled to a vaccine in the UK.

Travel

The COVID-19 pandemic continues to affect international travel.
No travel is risk-free, and many countries have closed their borders or restricted entry to UK travellers.
Any country may further restrict travel or bring in new rules at short notice, for example due to a new COVID-19 variant.
Read the guidance for your personal circumstances before deciding whether you should travel abroad.
